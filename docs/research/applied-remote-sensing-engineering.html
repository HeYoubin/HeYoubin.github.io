Applied Remote Sensing Research

This section summarizes public, research-oriented remote sensing application experience. Detailed operational engineering projects are now described in the Experience section using desensitized and public-safe language.

This page uses public-safe descriptions only. Internal source code, client information, private implementation details, non-public system screenshots, contract information, and non-public data are not disclosed.

Research-oriented Application Themes

Satellite Data Preprocessing and Analysis-ready Products

I have worked with multi-source satellite observations in workflows that connect raw imagery, preprocessing, quality control, and standardized analysis-ready products. Public-safe examples include radiometric calibration, atmospheric correction, geometric correction, image fusion, format conversion, and batch product preparation for downstream environmental monitoring tasks.

Fire-related Environmental Observation

My applied remote sensing experience includes wildfire-relevant environmental monitoring, thermal anomaly interpretation, time-series surface-condition products, and regional raster statistics. This theme connects naturally with my academic research on wildfire risk modeling, fire ecology, and explainable GeoAI.

Agricultural Monitoring and Crop Growth Assessment

I have contributed to agricultural remote sensing workflows involving vegetation indicators, crop growth assessment, anomaly analysis, thematic mapping, and county-level yield-estimation prototypes. The public research value lies in linking satellite time-series information, meteorological context, crop distribution, and statistical records for interpretable agricultural monitoring.

From Engineering Practice to Research Questions

These applied experiences help me formulate research questions that are both technically grounded and environmentally meaningful: how to improve the reliability of multi-source satellite products, how to integrate remote sensing indicators with machine learning, and how to make spatial models more interpretable for decision support.
