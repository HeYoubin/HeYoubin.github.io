Professional Experience Overview

Remote Sensing Engineering, Forestry Geospatial Analysis, and Operational Environmental Monitoring

I am a remote sensing algorithm engineer with professional experience across forestry geospatial data processing, multi-source satellite data preprocessing, operational remote sensing product generation, wildfire and environmental monitoring, agricultural remote sensing, and crop yield estimation.

My professional work has gradually evolved from forestry GIS, photogrammetry, point cloud visualization, and WebGIS development to more advanced remote sensing engineering tasks, including satellite data preprocessing, automated product generation, crop growth assessment, wildfire-related monitoring, thematic map automation, and operational algorithm deployment.

My current technical focus lies at the intersection of remote sensing, geospatial automation, wildfire monitoring, agricultural monitoring, and GeoAI-oriented environmental analysis.

Due to confidentiality requirements, detailed code, non-public datasets, private implementation details, client-specific materials, and operational product examples are not publicly displayed. The content below summarizes generalized technical workflows and my personal contributions.

Professional Focus

My professional experience can be summarized into five connected directions:

1. Forestry GIS and Geospatial Data Engineering

My early professional experience was rooted in forestry technical engineering, where I worked on geospatial data processing, forestry inventory support, photogrammetric workflows, individual tree segmentation, point cloud visualization, and WebGIS-related development.

This experience helped me build a practical foundation in:

  • GIS data organization
  • forest resource assessment workflows
  • photogrammetry and forestry spatial data processing
  • individual tree crown detection and segmentation
  • LiDAR and 3D point cloud visualization
  • WebGIS front-end components and interactive spatial visualization

This forestry-oriented foundation later supported my transition into broader remote sensing algorithm development and environmental monitoring systems.

2. Multi-source Satellite Data Processing

I have developed preprocessing workflows for multiple satellite sensors and data formats, including GF-series PMS imagery, Sentinel-2 MSI, AQUA/MODIS, FY-series satellites, and VIIRS-related fire-monitoring data.

These workflows involve:

  • Radiometric calibration
  • Atmospheric correction
  • Geometric correction
  • HDF parsing
  • Brightness temperature retrieval
  • Cloud and quality control
  • Pan-sharpening
  • Raster reprojection
  • Batch GeoTIFF product generation

This experience strengthened my ability to transform raw satellite observations into analysis-ready products for downstream monitoring and modeling tasks.

3. Operational Remote Sensing Product Generation

I have participated in operational remote sensing projects that convert satellite data into routine monitoring products. These systems support automated generation of raster products, regional statistics, comparison products, JSON outputs, thematic maps, structured records, and task-reporting results.

Typical workflows include:

  • Daily, dekadal, monthly, seasonal, and annual product generation
  • Administrative-region clipping
  • Land-cover and crop-type masking
  • Long-term baseline comparison
  • Raster statistics and JSON output
  • QGIS-based thematic map automation
  • Backend and client-side algorithm adaptation
  • Local deployment and operational reporting

4. Wildfire and Environmental Monitoring

Several of my professional and research projects focus on wildfire-related monitoring and environmental risk assessment. These include satellite fire hotspot preprocessing, thermal anomaly visualization, MODIS land surface temperature workflows, polar-orbiting satellite preprocessing, fire-point matching, and multi-source environmental factor processing.

My wildfire-related work connects:

  • Thermal infrared remote sensing
  • Fire hotspot interpretation
  • Satellite data preprocessing
  • Land surface temperature monitoring
  • Lightning-induced wildfire risk analysis
  • Machine-learning-based wildfire risk modeling
  • Explainable GeoAI methods

5. Agricultural Remote Sensing and Crop Yield Estimation

I have also developed crop growth assessment and county-level crop yield estimation workflows. These projects integrate FY-series satellite data, vegetation index products, crop distribution maps, meteorological variables, soil-related variables, historical yield records, and county boundaries.

My work includes:

  • NDVI, LAI, FVC, VHI, GCI, and brightness-related product generation
  • Daily, dekadal, and monthly crop growing-season monitoring
  • Anomaly and deviation-based crop growth assessment
  • Crop distribution masking
  • County-level crop area statistics
  • Regression and machine-learning-based yield modeling
  • Feature importance and multicollinearity analysis
