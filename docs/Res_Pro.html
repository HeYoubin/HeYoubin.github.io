Selected Research Projects

Multi-scale Wildfire Driving Factor Modeling in Southwest China

Keywords: Wildfire Risk Modeling, GeoAI, XGBoost, SHAP, GeoShapley, Spatial Machine Learning, Multi-scale Analysis, Remote Sensing

Research Question: How do multi-source geospatial factors shape the spatiotemporal patterns of grassland and forest fires in Southwest China, and how can explainable machine learning improve interpretation of wildfire risk?

Data: Multi-source geospatial and remote sensing datasets related to fire occurrence, environmental conditions, vegetation, terrain, and human activity.

Methods: Multi-scale geospatial modeling with machine learning and explainable AI methods, including XGBoost, SHAP, and potentially GeoShapley.

Key Findings / Expected Contributions: The project aims to improve understanding of nonlinear and spatially heterogeneous wildfire risk drivers. The manuscript, Analysis of Spatiotemporal Driving Factors of Grassland and Forest Fires in Southwest China Based on a Multi-Scale Geospatial Approach, is under review.

My Role: Project design, geospatial data preparation, modeling workflow, interpretation, visualization, and manuscript development.

Outputs: Manuscript under review.

Operational Remote Sensing Fire and Agricultural Monitoring Systems

Keywords: Remote Sensing Engineering, MODIS, FY-3D/E/F, FY-4B, GaoFen, AQUA, L1-to-L2 Processing, Fire Monitoring, Agricultural Monitoring

Research Question: How can production-oriented remote sensing workflows support reliable fire monitoring, agricultural monitoring, and satellite product generation?

Data: MODIS, FY-3D/E/F, FY-4B, GaoFen-series GF-1/2/6, and AQUA satellite data.

Methods: Python-based preprocessing algorithms for radiometric calibration, atmospheric correction, geometric correction, image fusion, and automated batch L1-to-L2 product generation.

Key Findings / Expected Contributions: The work supports surface temperature inversion, time-series compositing, thematic map production, vegetation index inversion, crop-growth assessment, and crop-yield estimation.

My Role: Remote Sensing Algorithm Engineer, May 2024 to present; algorithm development, workflow testing, product automation, and independent development of a crop-yield estimation module.

Outputs: Operational remote sensing workflows and production-oriented algorithm modules. Internal implementation details are not disclosed.

Multi-source Remote Sensing for Ecological Environment Assessment

Keywords: Landsat 8, Sentinel-2, dNBR, RSEI, Burned Area Extraction, Ecological Degradation Assessment, Land-use Classification

Research Question: How can multi-source remote sensing data quantify land-use change, burned areas, and post-fire ecological impacts?

Data: Preprocessed Landsat 8 time-series, Sentinel-2 imagery, field survey reference information, and ecological remote sensing indicators.

Methods: Supervised classification for five land-use categories, dNBR-based burned-area extraction, and RSEI-based ecological assessment.

Key Findings / Expected Contributions: The analysis quantified 103.5 km² expansion of built-up areas and estimated approximately 825 ha of burned area, within 4% of the 791.6 ha field survey reference. RSEI analysis showed that excellent ecological zones declined by 38.2%, and vegetation cover dropped by 3.3% after fire disturbance.

My Role: Data preprocessing, classification, burned-area extraction, ecological index analysis, validation, and result interpretation.

Outputs: One first-author publication, one second-author publication, and an award in a university remote sensing competition.

Remote Sensing Scene Classification with ECA-ResNet34

Keywords: Deep Learning, Remote Sensing Scene Classification, ECA Attention, ResNet-34, AID Dataset, PyTorch

Research Question: Can channel attention improve deep-learning-based remote sensing scene classification?

Data: AID remote sensing scene classification dataset.

Methods: Efficient Channel Attention blocks were integrated into ResNet-34 residual modules and validated on the AID dataset.

Key Findings / Expected Contributions: The model achieved 94.95% classification accuracy, improving accuracy by 3.45 percentage points compared with standard ResNet-34.

My Role: Model design, PyTorch implementation, experiment validation, and paper writing.

Outputs: One first-author IEEE conference publication.
